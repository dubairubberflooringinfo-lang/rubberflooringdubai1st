Understanding Rubber Flooring: The Basics

Rubber flooring might sound simple, but it's a powerhouse material made from natural or synthetic rubber. It's designed to handle heavy use while keeping things safe and comfortable. In Dubai, where temperatures soar and sand is everywhere, this flooring shines because it's tough against wear and tear.

What makes it special? For starters, it's flexible and resilient. Unlike hard tiles that crack under pressure, rubber bounces back‚Äîliterally. This resilience comes from its composition, often including recycled materials for an eco-friendly touch.

In terms of weather resistance, rubber doesn't absorb moisture like wood or carpet. It repels water, making it ideal for Dubai's occasional rain or high humidity. Plus, it withstands UV rays without fading quickly, ensuring your outdoor spaces look fresh for years.

Longevity is another key perk. With proper care, rubber floors can last over 15 years, outpacing many alternatives. That's a big win in a city where renovations can be costly and disruptive.
